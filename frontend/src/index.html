    Smart Charging

    Smart charging means adapting the charging cycle of EVs to both the conditions of the power system and the needs of vehicle users. This facilitates the integration of EVs while meeting mobility needs.

    Introduction

    With the term Smart Charging refer to a system able to monitor, manage and eventually limit the use of charging devices with the aim of optimizing energy consumption. The adjective “smart” underlines how this solution is able to adapt, in an intelligent and flexible way, the charging strategy to both the needs of the user of the vehicle and the power grid.

    • A Smart Charging solution requires a connection and the exchange of data between the electric vehicle, the charging station and the electricity supplier. Dedicated and optimized software algorithms running on the cloud allow to achieve an efficient use of the power grid by knowing, in real time, the available and required power level at any connected charging station. Due to its flexibility, this solution is therefore able to handle high peaks in power demand. It should also be noted that the charging period can be easily chosen or adapted, since most vehicles remain stationary for 90% of their time. The OCPP data exchange protocol (OCPP stands for Open Charge Point Protocol): it is a standard protocol that defines the communication between EV, charging station and a central management system and is a reference for implementing EV smart charging solutions.
    • We can identify five main benefits of Smart Charging:
    • 1. efficient and optimal use of the power grid;
    • 2. it is a solution able to avoid the congestion of the power network;
    • 3. it is capable of smoothing peaks in power demand;
    • 4. it can produce electricity directly on sitethat is, in the charging stations) exploiting renewable energy sources such as solar panels, wind energy, and other;
    • 5. investments in grid infrastructure can be reduced.
    • Smart EV charging management system utilizing charging strategy that includes effective reservation management and efficient slot allocation of charging stations. Considering composite cost that includes waiting time, estimated charging time, estimated charging cost, user discontent factor and charging station congestion impact in such a method, our scheduling scheme shall furnish a set of optimal solutions.
